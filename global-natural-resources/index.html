Natural Resources

Global Natural Resource Mapping

These platforms provide open data on natural resources and physical geography at a global scale — soil composition, vegetation patterns, terrain elevation, forest cover, and water systems mapped with satellite imagery and machine learning.

Global Platforms

Open-Source Resource Data

These platforms aggregate environmental and natural resource data from around the world, providing free access to datasets covering soil, vegetation, climate, topography, and ecosystems.

OpenLandMap

An open-source project providing global maps of soil, vegetation, and climate variables using machine learning and crowdsourced data.

Visit Site

Natural Earth

A public domain map dataset that includes tightly integrated vector and raster data for global administrative boundaries and physical features like rivers and lakes.

Visit Site

NASA Earthdata

Provides unrestricted access to petabytes of Earth science data, including forest cover, fire activity, and surface water monitoring.

Visit Site

OpenTopography

A community resource specifically for high-resolution, open-source LiDAR and topography data across the globe.

Visit Site

UNEP Environmental Data Explorer

The authoritative source for UN global datasets on ecosystems, disasters, and climate.

Visit Site
Agricultural Data

United States & International Data

The U.S. government maintains some of the most granular open data for global and domestic produce pricing — from daily terminal market reports to county-level farm statistics. At the global scale, FAO provides comprehensive agricultural production datasets for nearly every country.

USDA Market News — Specialty Crops

A primary source for daily terminal market prices, shipping point data, and movement trends for fruits, vegetables, and specialty crops across the United States.

Visit Site

My Market News

A public data portal for searching terminal market reports, including specific data for international terminals like Toronto. Offers API access for large dataset retrieval.

Visit Site

USDA Quick Stats

The most comprehensive database for U.S. agricultural statistics — over 57 million records covering farm locations by county, crop yields, and prices received by farmers.

Visit Site

Weekly Retail Specialty Crops Report

Tracks advertised prices from major grocery retailers on a weekly basis, offering insight into what consumers pay compared to farm and wholesale prices.

Visit Site

FAOSTAT

The Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations provides massive open datasets on global agricultural production, land use, and producer prices for almost every country.

Visit Site
Reference

Key Data Types for Resources

When searching these portals, look for these specific "layers" or products to find resource-specific data.

CanVec

A digital topographic reference product for Canada, offering vector data on roads, hydrology, landforms, and infrastructure.

Hydrographic Data

Information for water resource management and flow modeling — essential for environmental assessments and infrastructure planning.

Enhanced Forest Inventory

High-resolution data used for sustainable forest management, combining satellite imagery with ground-truth measurements.
