Canadian Geospatial Intelligence

For mapping and data similar to the National Elevation Data Strategy, these primary Canadian sources provide high-resolution geospatial information across land, water, infrastructure, soil, and climate — aggregated from federal, provincial, and territorial agencies.

Geospatial Platforms

Canada's Open Data Gateways

These portals are the primary access points for Canada's open geospatial information — from comprehensive federal datasets to specialized mapping and download tools.

GEO.ca

The definitive gateway for Canada's open geospatial information. Aggregates over 100,000 datasets — including land, water, and infrastructure — from federal, provincial, and territorial sources.

Visit Site

Toporama

A dynamic mapping tool by the Atlas of Canada that allows you to view topographic data — relief, drainage, and roads — at national, regional, and local scales.

Visit Site

GeoGratis

A portal specifically for downloading free geospatial data, including satellite imagery, National Topographic System (NTS) maps, and prepackaged elevation datasets.

Visit Site

The Atlas of Canada

Focuses on the country's environment, society, and economy, offering thematic maps on subjects like minerals, energy, and land cover.

Visit Site

Canadian Soil Information Service

The national archive for soil and land resource information, providing datasets on ecological groupings and soil properties across Canada.

Visit Site

ClimateData.ca

Provides historical and projected climate data visualizations for any location in Canada to help with environmental planning and risk assessment.

Visit Site
Agricultural Data

Canadian Agricultural Data

Canada provides extensive open data through federal and provincial portals, focusing on both production and market value — from farm-gate pricing and cultivated area to daily wholesale and terminal market reports.

Statistics Canada — Fruits & Vegetables Survey

Provides estimates for cultivated area, production, and farm-gate value — the price received by farmers at the first point of sale — for fruits and vegetables across Canada.

Visit Site

Open Government Canada

Hosts datasets on commercial vegetable production, specifically tracking average prices and farm values across Canadian provinces and territories.

Visit Site

InfoHort — Agriculture & Agri-Food Canada

Offers daily wholesale-to-retail market price reports for terminal markets in Toronto and Montreal, detailing asking prices for various commodities and origins.

Visit Site

Ontario Food Terminal (OFTB)

Known as the "stock exchange" for produce in Canada — the largest wholesale fruit and produce distribution centre in Canada and third-largest in North America, where prices fluctuate daily based on supply and demand.
